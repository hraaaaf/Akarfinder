# AkarFinder City Marks — Fidelity Reference Pack

## Purpose

City Marks are proprietary AkarFinder identity illustrations, but unlike abstract service/property illustrations they must remain faithful to real city landmarks.

Rule: **reference first, vector second.**

A city mark is not production-ready until its silhouette is recognizable without the city label.

## Shared style

- AkarFinder navy / blue / light-blue / white only
- no bronze, gold or champagne
- flat geometric simplification
- no tourist clip-art
- no invented skyline
- no flags
- no generic lighthouse/minaret/fortress substitutions
- same baseline, optical weight and detail density across all cities

## Casablanca

### Locked anchors
- Mosquée Hassan II minaret and mosque massing
- Casablanca Finance City contemporary skyline

### Silhouette invariants
- Hassan II minaret: dominant tall square shaft, characteristic crown/lantern profile
- mosque mass sits low and broad beside the minaret
- CFC appears as a separate modern vertical cluster, not merged into the mosque

### Forbidden shortcuts
- generic mosque dome
- generic skyscrapers with no CFC identity
- minaret proportions invented from memory

## Rabat

### Locked anchors
- Prince Moulay Abdellah stadium complex
- Mohammed VI Tower

### Silhouette invariants
- Mohammed VI Tower: very slender tapering/curved vertical profile with distinctive pointed crown
- stadium: broad low bowl/roof mass, clearly separated from tower

### Forbidden shortcuts
- substituting Grand Théâtre for the stadium
- rectangular generic skyscraper
- football iconography replacing the actual stadium architecture

## Marrakech

### Locked anchor
- Koutoubia minaret

### Silhouette invariants
- tall rectangular Almohad tower
- crenellated upper profile
- lantern/dome/finial crown
- tower remains the dominant form; palms may be secondary only

### Forbidden shortcuts
- generic Moroccan minaret
- exaggerated domes
- tourist postcard collage

## Fès

### Locked anchor
- Bab Boujloud

### Silhouette invariants
- large central horseshoe arch
- two smaller lateral arches
- horizontal rectangular facade
- crenellated parapet
- central arch clearly dominates the composition

### Forbidden shortcuts
- generic medina gate
- single arch only
- excessive zellige detail that destroys small-size readability

## Tanger

### Locked identity
- Kasbah / medina fortress profile
- relationship to the bay

### Silhouette invariants
- white/fortified kasbah massing with crenellated profile
- layered hill/medina relationship
- bay/horizon used as a secondary geographic cue

### Forbidden shortcuts
- generic lighthouse
- generic castle
- Cape Spartel icon used as sole city identity

## Agadir

### Locked identity
- Kasbah Oufella
- crescent-shaped bay

### Silhouette invariants
- hilltop kasbah wall/tower profile
- clear elevated relationship above city/bay
- bay shown as one clean sweeping curve

### Forbidden shortcuts
- invented modern skyline
- beach umbrella/palm tourism pictograms
- kasbah drawn as a generic medieval castle

## Production gate per city

1. collect 3–5 verified reference views;
2. identify silhouette invariants;
3. produce monochrome silhouette draft;
4. compare against real references;
5. simplify into AkarFinder blue system;
6. validate at 64 px, 128 px and card size;
7. only then replace city photography where UX review confirms the mark is superior.

## Integration rule

Current photography stays live until the corresponding city mark passes the gate. Fès is added to the target city family even though it was not in the original five-photo homepage set.
